## METHODOLOGY

### Source Verification
- **Al-Nahhas**: *Al-Nasikh wa-l-Mansukh fi al-Quran* (published scholarly edition, Dar al-Kutub al-Ilmiyyah)
- **Ibn al-Jawzi**: *Nasikh al-Quran* (classical authority on naskh)
- **Al-Suyuti**: *Al-Itqan fi Ulum al-Quran*, Vol. 2 (comprehensive reference)
- **Burton, John**: *The Sources of Islamic Law: Islamic Theories of Abrogation* (Edinburgh University Press, 1990)

### Consensus Scoring Methodology
- **0.95**: Unanimous agreement across all four Sunni madhabs + classical consensus
- **0.90**: Agreement across 3-4 madhabs + strong classical support
- **0.85**: Agreement across 2-3 madhabs + moderate classical support
- **0.80**: Majority madhab agreement or scholarly consensus with some dissent
- **0.70-0.75**: Moderate agreement; some scholarly dissent recorded
- **0.60**: Disputed; significant scholarly debate or context interpretation questions

### Classification System
- **EXPLICIT (Naskh Sarih)**: Clear textual abrogation with scholarly consensus
- **IMPLIED (Naskh Dimni)**: Inferred from context or contradiction resolution
- **PARTIAL (Naskh Juz'i)**: Abrogation of specific aspect or condition, not entire verse
