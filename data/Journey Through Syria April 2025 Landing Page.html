A Journey Through "New" Syria

April 2025

From Daraa to Idlib, witnessing resilience and hope after the fall of Assad.

"O people... We have made you peoples and tribes that you may know one another." (Quran 49:13)

Daraa: Jbab Village

Arrived at 2 AM under a bridge to join my friend Salam for his brother’s wedding.

Witnessed Syrian hospitality despite 2-3 hours of daily electricity. Met Imam, a young leader launching an educational movement post-Assad.

Damascus: A Changed Capital

Walked through the old city, Hamidiyah market, and Umayyad Mosque—saw the impact of sanctions since my 2009 visit.

Public transport now accessible post-liberation. Met a Christian doctor joyful about newfound freedom.

Newfound Freedom

Ghouta & Duma: Scars and Recovery

In Ghouta, people once ate grass to survive. Duma, despite destruction, is one of the cleanest cities—rebuilding slowly.

"Despite all Duma has been through, I found a fantastic soul."

Rebuilding Slowly

Idlib: A Rebel Capital

Supported by Turkey, Idlib has better infrastructure—cleaner, safer. Turkish lira and USDT everywhere.

Saw women walking alone at night, defying stereotypes of extremism. Lower cost of living, better services.

Extremely Safe
Turkish Lira & USDT

Aleppo: Emptiness and Hope

The old city around the Citadel is hauntingly empty—destroyed by war and earthquake.

Modern parts show signs of life: youth hanging out, clean hospitals. A Christian vendor at Abu George felt safe post-liberation.

Signs of Life

Border Crossing: Syrian-Lebanese Challenges

Walked across the Syrian border, stamped out with smiles. Hitched rides to Lebanese immigration.

Faced restrictions as a Moroccan passport holder—24-hour transit permit only. Met kindness from locals despite hurdles.

Local Kindness

Reflections: Resilience & Solidarity

No extreme poverty despite hardship—Syrians help each other. Palestinian cause ever-present in conversations.

"Come see us in five years! We’ll be great, inshallah."

Shared social innovation ideas to support rebuilding—a journey of connection and hope.

No Extreme Poverty
