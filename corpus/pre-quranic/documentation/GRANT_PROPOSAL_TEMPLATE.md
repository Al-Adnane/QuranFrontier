## Project Summary (Abstract)

**Title:** Pre-Quranic Sacred Texts: Digital Preservation and Scholarly Verification

**Summary:**
This project will create a verified, open-access digital corpus of sacred texts from 36+ pre-Quranic religious traditions. The collection includes texts in 40+ languages dating from 60,000 BCE to 700 CE, representing humanity's diverse religious heritage before the rise of Islam.

While a preliminary framework has been created, the content requires scholarly verification, community consultation, and technical enhancement to meet academic standards. This funding will support:

1. Expert verification of all texts against critical editions
2. Community consultation for living traditions
3. Technical infrastructure for access and preservation
4. Outreach and training for scholars and communities

**Impact:** This resource will serve researchers in religious studies, linguistics, digital humanities, and comparative theology, while respecting the sovereignty of living religious communities over their sacred knowledge.

---

## Statement of Need

### The Problem

1. **Fragmented Resources:** Pre-Quranic sacred texts are scattered across hundreds of critical editions, many behind paywalls or in specialized libraries.

2. **Endangered Languages:** Many texts are in endangered or extinct languages (Avestan, Cuneiform, Hieroglyphs) requiring specialized knowledge.

3. **Living Tradition Concerns:** Indigenous and traditional religious communities often lack control over how their sacred knowledge is represented digitally.

4. **Verification Gap:** Existing digital collections often lack scholarly verification or community input.

### The Opportunity

Digital humanities methods enable:
- Unified access to diverse traditions
- Computational analysis across traditions
- Preservation of endangered language texts
- Community-controlled access protocols

## Project Goals and Objectives

### Goal 1: Scholarly Verification

**Objective 1.1:** Verify all 40 JSON text files against critical editions
- Hire subject matter experts for each tradition
- Create verification reports for each file
- Document all corrections made

**Objective 1.2:** Establish scholarly advisory board
- Recruit 10+ experts across traditions
- Hold quarterly review meetings
- Implement peer review process

### Goal 2: Community Consultation

**Objective 2.1:** Contact all living tradition communities
- Yoruba/Ifá practitioners
- Buddhist organizations
- Hindu communities
- Indigenous groups (Native American, Aboriginal, etc.)

**Objective 2.2:** Implement community feedback
- Remove restricted content as requested
- Add access controls where needed
- Create community acknowledgment protocols

### Goal 3: Technical Infrastructure

**Objective 3.1:** Develop search and access platform
- Web-based interface
- Advanced search capabilities
- Multi-script rendering support

**Objective 3.2:** Ensure long-term preservation
- Deposit in trusted digital repository
- Create archival packages (OAIS compliant)
- Establish maintenance plan

### Goal 4: Outreach and Training

**Objective 4.1:** Disseminate to scholarly community
- Present at digital humanities conferences
- Publish in peer-reviewed journals
- Create citation guidelines

**Objective 4.2:** Train users
- Create documentation and tutorials
- Hold workshops for scholars
- Develop educational materials
